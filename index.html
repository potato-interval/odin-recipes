Odin Recipes

Appetizers:

  • Edamame: Steamed and salted soybean pods.
  • Goma-ae: Boiled spinach with seasame sauce.
  • Hiyayakko: Chilled tofu topped with grated ginger and green onions.
  • Takowasa: Raw octopus mixed with wasabi.

Salads:

  • Seaweed Salad: Mixed seaweeds with a light sesame dressing.
  • Sunomono: Cucumber salad with venegar dressing served with seafood.

Yakitori:

  • Negima: Skewers of chicken and green onion.
  • Tsukune: Grilled chicken meatballs with yakitori sauce.
  • Gyutan: Grilled beef tongue.
  • Shishito Peppers: Grilled shishito peppers with a sprinkle of sea salt.

Deep-Fried Dishes:

  • Karaage: Japanese-style fried chicken.
  • Tempura: Lightly battered and fried vegetables and shrimp.
  • Tonkatsu: Breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet.

Stewed Dishes:

  • Oden: A type of hot pot with various ingredient such as boiled eggs, daikon, konjac, and processed fishcakes stewed in soy-flavored dashi broth.
  • Nikujaga: Meat and potatoes stewed in sweet soy sause.

Rice and Noodle Dishes:

  • Yaki Onigiri: Grilled rice balls brushed with soy sauce.
  • Zaru Soba: Cold buckwheat noodles served with a dipping sauce.
  • Chahan: Japanese-style fried rice with small pieces of vegetables and protein.

Desserts:

  • Mochi: Japanese rice cake made of mochigome filled with sweet red bean paste.
  • Matcha Ice Cream: Green tea flavored ice cream.
  • Dorayaki: A dessert made of two small pancake-like patties with a filling of sweet Azuki red bean paste.

Beverages:

  • Sake: Japanese rice wine.
  • Shochu: A Japanese distilled beverage, usuallu made from barley, sweet potatoes, or rice.
  • Beer: Various Japanese beers like Asahi, Sapporo, and Kirin.
  • Matcha: A type of powdered green tea that's traditionally used in Japanese tea ceremonies. It has a vibrant green color and a distinctive, slightly bitter flavour.
